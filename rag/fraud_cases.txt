Case 1:
Large transactions from a new device shortly after password change
often indicate account takeover fraud.

Case 2:
Multiple transactions in different geographic locations within a
short period of time can signal card cloning or compromised accounts.

Case 3:
High value electronics purchases are common in fraud schemes
because they can be resold quickly.

Case 4:
Transactions occurring at unusual hours combined with
large transaction amounts may indicate automated fraud attempts.

Case 5:
Rapid sequence of transactions from different merchants
can signal testing of stolen credit card numbers.
